Abstract

The overall goal for this project was to create a viable solution that could be used by Etsy sellers in order to gain an upper hand. This prototype has gone through numerous stages of planning, development, and refinement. There are numerous concepts and principles that were implemented under the umbrella of Scenario Based Design (SBD) that allowed for an array of benefits compared to traditional methods of production. Looking through the lens of many different archetypes of users, I was able to identify given problem scenarios and act upon them to implement different features and structures in my system. From there, activity scenarios and claims were analyzed to create primitive, or “quick and dirty” prototypes also known as low fidelity prototypes. This allowed me to conduct formative tests in order to procure reactions from actual users. The summative goal of the system was to allow Etsy sellers to research keywords that they wanted their items to show up during a given search on the Etsy marketplace. Therefore, they would gain exposure and sales resulting in a revenue increase. The use of keywords is very important within the Etsy search algorithm, that is why the company has allowed users to input up to 13 keywords directly into listings. This has been shown to increase sales and exposure, leading to the need of optimized keywords. To solve that problem, I found a way to scrape keywords from top listings for a given keyword in the marketplace so that the user will have the best (and most optimal) chance at registering for a top spot.
